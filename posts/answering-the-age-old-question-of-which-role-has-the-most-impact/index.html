League of legends is a team focused esport. This should not be shocking or anything like that, everyone knows how much team work matters in League. One player falling behind can set the whole team back whole side objectives worth of progress. Games are lost on players falling behind, and games are won on every player pulling their weight. However, League of legends is also well known for being a complicated game, part of this is because of how different the five roles are. So with every player being very important, and every player playing a completely different role, this naturally leads to everyone’s competitive spirit coming out. So the question arises, which role is the most important in a match? Out of the five roles, which one holds the most impact? First things first, in order to determine which role has the most impact, looking at individual role stats is most important, primary and secondary objectives are tied to the team as a whole so they aren’t helpful here. Second, The League of Legends esport is very large with lots of players ranging across many skill levels, so keep the playing field as even as possible, only players who participated at Worlds 2025 will be looked at. So, looking at performance for each role.

The three key stats to look at are kills, deaths, and assists. This looks at how good each role is at playing on its own, playing with the team, and staying alive. Some obvious things to point out, Support and Jungle have lots of assists, and Support has very few kills across the board as well. These roles are both much more focused on playing with the team and enabling them to do well, so their stats are naturally not too impressive to see. The big outlier here is ADC, they have a great spread with lots of kills and few deaths. But, ADCs spend the most time out of everyone on their team playing with the Support, so how many of their kills are due to their own role and not Support?

Code 
killers <- worlds25 |>
  group_by(Position) |>
  summarize(
    total = sum(SoloKills)
  )

rows <- 10

worldwaffle <- killers |>
  uncount(total) |>
  mutate(
    x = (row_number() - 1) %/% rows + 1,
    y = (row_number() - 1) %% rows + 1
  ) |>
  ungroup()


ggplot() +
  geom_tile(
    data=worldwaffle,
    aes(
      x = x, y = y, fill = Position
      ),
    color = "white",
    linewidth = .5) +
  coord_equal() +
  theme_void() +
  labs(
    x = "1 square = 1 solo kill",
    title="Top and mid laners perform great when alone", 
    subtitle="A solo kill requires no other teammates to be present, which some roles depend on.",
    caption="Source: Game of Legends eSports | Graphic by Kavanaugh Splittgerber",
    fill = "Record") + 
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 16, face = "bold"),
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size = 10),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 8),
    axis.title.y = element_blank(),
    strip.text.x = element_text(size = 6, hjust=0)
  )

Well well well, when we look at solo kills, ADCs look a lot less impressive. Obviously Jungle and Support still don’t look great as they are team players first and independent players second. Top and Mid spend the most time in 1v1 scenarios compared to other roles so when looking at how good they perform independently, it puts them back in the running with ADCs. Kills are obviously important, but how much effort it takes to earn a kill is also something to consider. A squishy Support is going to be a lot easier to take down then a tanky Top laner, so a solo kill against a weak target is a lot less impressive. Luckily, tracking the amount of effort each role puts into earning kills is something that can be tracked.

Who is pulling the most weight? It’s the ADCs. So even though ADCs don’t get the most solo kills, they are still putting in the majority of effort when they do get kills. So in conclusion, ADC is definitely the most impactful role in League of Legend. ADCs may need support to do the best they can do, but without an ADC, the team loses their biggest threat. The team’s playmaker is the ADC which sadly means that all of the ADC players who act like they are the most important member of the team might be right.
